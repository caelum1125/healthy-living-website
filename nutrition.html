Understanding Macronutrients

Macronutrients are the nutrients your body needs in large amounts to provide energy and maintain bodily functions.

Carbohydrates

Carbohydrates are the body's primary source of energy. Complex carbs provide fiber and lasting fuel.

Whole food sources: oats, quinoa, brown rice, sweet potatoes, fruits, vegetables, legumes

Proteins

Proteins are essential for building and repairing tissues, including muscle, skin, and hormones.

Whole food sources: eggs, chicken, tofu, lentils, beans, Greek yogurt, cottage cheese

Fats

Fats help absorb vitamins, support cell growth, and serve as long-term energy storage.

Whole food sources: avocados, olive oil, nuts, seeds, fatty fish (salmon, mackerel)

Micronutrients & Their Functions

  • Iron — supports oxygen transport in the blood. Sources: red meat, spinach, lentils, chickpeas
  • Calcium — builds strong bones and teeth. Sources: dairy, leafy greens, almonds
  • Vitamin D — boosts immunity and bone health. Sources: sunlight, salmon, egg yolks, mushrooms
  • Magnesium — aids muscle and nerve function. Sources: dark chocolate, avocados, nuts, legumes
  • Vitamin B12 — supports brain health and red blood cells. Sources: eggs, meat, dairy, fortified cereals
  • Potassium — regulates fluid balance and heart function. Sources: bananas, potatoes, beans, yogurt

Common Nutrient Deficiencies

Nutrient Whole Food Sources Deficiency Effects
Protein Eggs, chicken, lentils, tofu, Greek yogurt Muscle loss, fatigue, immune weakness
Iron Red meat, spinach, legumes, fortified cereals Anemia, tiredness, brain fog
Vitamin D Sunlight, salmon, egg yolks, mushrooms Weak bones, low mood, poor immunity
